5 Ways to Make Dog Toys Last Longer

Tired of toys falling apart in days? These five easy strategies help you stretch each purchase, keep playtime exciting, and make sure your pup always has something safe to chew.

  • Match the toy to your dog’s play style

    Choose tougher materials for heavy chewers and softer options for gentle nibblers to prevent unnecessary wear.

  • Rotate toys every few days

    Swapping toys keeps them exciting and gives each one a break, which slows down fraying and squeaker damage.

  • Use supervised play for tug and fetch

    Interactive play helps you spot early tears and stop overzealous chewing before toys shred.

  • Store toys in a dry, clean bin

    Keeping toys off the floor and away from moisture reduces mildew, odor, and material breakdown.

  • Clean toys on a schedule

    Washing plush and rubber toys removes grit that can grind fibers down, extending their life.

Consistent toy care keeps playtime fresh and saves you money over time. Mix these habits into your routine so your dog’s favorites last longer and stay safe.
