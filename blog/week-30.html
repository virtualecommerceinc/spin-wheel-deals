How Play Intensity Affects Recovery and Sleep

How Play Intensity Affects Recovery and Sleep because health and safety guide everything we choose for our dogs. This week covers practical considerations to keep playtime safe, hygienic, and beneficial for overall wellness.

Quick Take (TL;DR)

  • Toy safety isn't one-time—it's an ongoing inspection habit
  • Material choice affects digestion, dental health, and allergies
  • Rotation and cleaning prevent bacterial buildup
  • Age and health status change toy suitability
  • Senior dogs and puppies have different toy needs than healthy adults

Buyer Checklist

Before you buy, confirm you can check these boxes:

  • You inspect toys weekly for damage before use
  • Toys are cleaned regularly (weekly wash minimum)
  • You know what materials your dog can safely chew
  • All toys are appropriate size (no choking hazards)
  • You have a plan for toys your dog might be allergic to
  • Toys are stored in a clean, accessible place

How to Use This Week's Picks

Start by identifying which toy matches your dog's size, chew intensity, and play style. Rotate toys every few days to maintain novelty and prevent overuse damage. Inspect each toy weekly for cracks, loose seams, or missing pieces. Replace immediately if you find safety concerns. Most dogs show preference for 1–2 toys; use the others as backups or recovery toys when the favorite is in the wash.
